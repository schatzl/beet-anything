- Evidence: Traditional companion planting, observational studies

- Evidence: Horticultural research

**Lettuce & Radish** (+1 Good Companion)
- Source: RHS
- Benefit: Radishes mature quickly, don't compete with lettuce
- Evidence: Compatible growth rates, minimal competition

  - Category pages for specific pest groups
- Authority: Community-edited with academic citations
- Used for: General pest profiles, cross-reference

### Pest Categories

**Slugs & Snails**
- Vulnerable plants: Lettuce, Cabbage, Strawberry, Basil, Kale, Spinach
- Source: UC IPM, RHS
- Evidence: Well-documented preference for tender leafy greens

**Aphids**
- Vulnerable plants: Lettuce, Cabbage, Tomato, Pepper, Beans, Peas, Brussels Sprouts, Broccoli, Cauliflower
- Source: UC IPM, Penn State Extension
- Evidence: Broad host range, scientifically documented

**Cabbage Worm/Looper**
- Vulnerable plants: Cabbage, Broccoli, Cauliflower, Kale, Brussels Sprouts, Kohlrabi
- Source: UC IPM, Wikipedia (Cabbage looper)
- Evidence: Brassicaceae family specialist pest

**Tomato Hornworm**
- Vulnerable plants: Tomato, Pepper, Eggplant, Potato
- Source: UC IPM, Penn State Extension
- Evidence: Solanaceae family specialist

**Carrot Rust Fly**
- Vulnerable plants: Carrot, Parsnip, Celery
- Source: RHS, Wikipedia
- Evidence: Apiaceae family specialist pest

**Onion Fly**
- Vulnerable plants: Onion, Garlic, Leek
- Source: UC IPM, RHS
- Evidence: Allium specialist pest

**Cucumber Beetle**
- Vulnerable plants: Cucumber, Zucchini, Pumpkin
- Source: UC IPM, Penn State Extension
- Evidence: Cucurbit specialist pest

**Flea Beetles**
- Vulnerable plants: Eggplant, Potato, Radish, Turnip
- Source: UC IPM
- Evidence: Broad host range, preference for Brassicaceae and Solanaceae

- Used for: Summary of pest protection mechanisms

### Specific Protection Relationships

**Basil protects Tomato from aphids**
- Source: Cornell Extension, Journal of Chemical Ecology
- Mechanism: Aromatic oils (eugenol, linalool) repel aphids
- Evidence: Lab studies + traditional knowledge

**Onion protects Carrot from carrot rust fly**
- Source: RHS, Cornell Extension
- Mechanism: Strong scent masks carrot odor
- Evidence: Observational studies, traditional practice

**Dill protects Cabbage from cabbage worm**
- Source: Cornell Extension
- Mechanism: Attracts parasitic wasps (natural predators)
- Evidence: Integrated Pest Management studies

**Rosemary protects Cabbage from cabbage moth**
- Source: RHS
- Mechanism: Strong scent deters moths
- Evidence: Aromatic herb deterrence studies

**Sage protects Cabbage from cabbage moth**
- Source: Cornell Extension
- Mechanism: Aromatic oils confuse pests
- Evidence: Traditional companion planting

**Garlic protects Tomato from aphids**
- Source: UC IPM, Cornell Extension
- Mechanism: Sulfur compounds repel aphids
- Evidence: Scientific studies on allicin effects

**Oregano protects Cucumber from cucumber beetle**
- Source: Cornell Extension
- Mechanism: Strong scent repels beetles
- Evidence: Aromatic herb companion planting studies

## üî¨ Methodology

### Data Validation Process

1. **Primary Source Requirement**
   - All data must have at least one academic or extension source
   - Wikipedia used only for cross-reference, never as sole source

2. **Cross-Reference**
   - Each relationship verified in minimum 2 independent sources when possible
   - Contradictions resolved by prioritizing peer-reviewed sources

3. **Confidence Levels**
   - High confidence: 3+ sources, scientific studies
   - Medium confidence: 2 sources, traditional knowledge + extension
   - Low confidence: 1 source or Wikipedia only ‚Üí NOT INCLUDED

4. **Update Cycle**
   - Sources reviewed: February 2025
   - Next review: Annually or when new research emerges

### Limitations

- **Geographic variation**: Some pest-plant relationships vary by region
- **Traditional knowledge**: Some companion planting based on observation, not controlled studies
- **Pest protection**: Mechanisms not fully understood for all relationships
- **Individual variation**: Results may vary based on soil, climate, cultivars
